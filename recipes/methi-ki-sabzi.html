They aren't wrong, though: people can't really change some things about themselves. Six-year-old Ram Iyer didn't give a flying fuck about what his parents wanted, and I still don't.

So, with that out of the way, here's my mom's very simple but quite delicious fenugreek sabzi, with tomatoes for a touch of sweetness to temper the earthy taste, and garlic to add a smidgen of flavor to the heat of the chillies.

Ingredients

  • 1 bunch of fenugreek, de-stemmed and chopped
  • 3 green chillies, diced
  • 1 large tomatoes or 2 small ones, diced
  • Turmeric
  • Red chilly powder
  • 5-6 cloves of garlic, diced
  • 2 medium-sized onions, chopped fine
  • 2-3 teaspoons black mustard seeds

Method

  1. First, heat up a skillet on medium-to-high heat, and pour in 2-3 tablespoons of oil
  2. Quickly add the mustard seeds, and wait for them to pop
  3. When they've calmed down, add in the green chillies and onions. Stir.
  4. Wait for the onions to release water, then add in the garlic, tomatoes and fenugreek.
  5. Season with turmeric, sprinkle 3 teaspoons of salt, and 1 teaspoon of red chilly powder. Mix it thoroughly.
  6. Add half a small cup of water and cover the skillet to let it simmer.
  7. Check in after 8-10 minutes -- the fenugreek should cook quickly, but you don't want the sabzi to be watery; just a little moist.
  8. Serve hot with chapatis! Bone Apple Tea!

And there you have it! My mom's famous methi ki sabzi. It's admittedly quite simple, and I'm sure mom makes it much better than I do, but hey, I do what I can. Enjoy!
